About Our Group

Our research group studies memristors, neuromorphic computing, and multiphysics simulation methods for understanding resistive switching devices. We are especially interested in modeling the electrical, thermal, and ionic transport processes that control switching behavior in oxide-based memory devices.

Research Areas

  • ECM Memristor Modeling
  • VCM Electroforming Simulation
  • COMSOL Multiphysics
  • Open-source Software Development

Open-Source Software

We develop open-source Python software for simulating voltage-controlled memristive switching in oxide-based devices. The software allows users to explore device geometry, material parameters, voltage conditions, oxygen vacancy migration, conductive filament formation, temperature evolution, electric potential, and current-voltage behavior.

This project is designed to make memristor simulations more accessible for research, education, and future software development.
