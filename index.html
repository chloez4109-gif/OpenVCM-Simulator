About Us

Our research group focuses on modeling and simulation of memristive devices, especially electrochemical metallization memory and valence change memory systems. We study the physical mechanisms that control resistive switching, including ion migration, oxygen vacancy transport, Joule heating, electric-field effects, and conductive filament formation.

A major goal of our work is to better understand how nanoscale material and device properties influence switching behavior in memristors. These devices are important for future memory technologies, neuromorphic computing, and brain-inspired electronic systems.

Research Areas

  • ECM Memristor Modeling
  • VCM Electroforming Simulation
  • COMSOL Multiphysics Modeling
  • Open-source Software Development
  • Neuromorphic Computing Devices
