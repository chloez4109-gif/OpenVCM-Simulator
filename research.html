Overview

Our research investigates memristive devices, which are electronic devices whose resistance can change depending on their electrical history. These devices are promising for nonvolatile memory, reconfigurable electronics, and neuromorphic computing systems.

We use computational modeling to study how physical processes such as ion migration, oxygen vacancy redistribution, electric-field-driven transport, and Joule heating contribute to resistive switching behavior.

ECM Memristor Modeling

Electrochemical metallization memory devices switch through the movement of metal ions and the formation or dissolution of metallic conductive filaments. Our modeling work explores how electrical bias, ionic transport, and material properties influence filament growth and device conductance.

VCM Electroforming Simulation

Valence change memory devices often rely on oxygen vacancy migration inside an oxide layer. Under an applied voltage, oxygen vacancies redistribute and can form a conductive filament between the electrodes. This process changes the resistance state of the device.

Our VCM simulations focus on electroforming, conductive filament formation, temperature evolution, and current-voltage characteristics in oxide-based memristors.

COMSOL Multiphysics

We use COMSOL Multiphysics to study coupled electrical, thermal, and ionic transport processes. These models help connect device geometry, material parameters, and boundary conditions to the resulting switching behavior.

Multiphysics modeling is important because resistive switching is not controlled by only one effect. Electric field, temperature, conductivity, and vacancy concentration can all affect one another during device operation.

Neuromorphic Computing

Memristors are promising candidates for neuromorphic computing because their resistance states can be used to represent synaptic weights in brain-inspired hardware systems. Understanding and controlling memristor switching behavior is important for improving reliability, scalability, and energy efficiency in these systems.
